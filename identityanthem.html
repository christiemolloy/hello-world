What does the music that you listen to say about you?



Identity Anthem

Tools
HTML/CSS
User Research

This question has always startled me. People listen to music for hours each day, yet they find it hard to describe how they choose their music, as it is an emotional connection. So I designed a user research experiment to discover if the music that people listen to is telling of their identity. I asked twenty of my closest friends to anonymously submit their “identity anthem”: a playlist of ten songs that they believe described who they are. Everyday for two weeks I listened to one of the playlists and attempted to match it with a person. I got 75% of the results correct and found that it was easier to distinguish the male playlists from female ones, and Australian playlists from American ones. This was only an experiment from a small pool, so none of these findings are conclusive, however it is something I want to continue to research in the future. In response, I designed and developed this website to showcase my own “identity anthem”, and to practice the parallax scrolling technique.
